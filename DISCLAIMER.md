# Disclaimer

This MCP server provides Swedish food safety regulatory data for informational purposes only.

## Not Legal or Professional Advice

The data served by this tool does not constitute legal advice or professional food safety advice. Product requirements, hygiene rules, labelling rules, and raw milk regulations are based on published Swedish and EU regulatory sources, but individual food business operations require case-specific assessment. Always consult Livsmedelsverket or a qualified food safety advisor before starting food production or sales. Non-compliance with food safety regulations can result in enforcement action and product recalls.

## Data Sources

All data is sourced from:

- **Livsmedelsverket** (Swedish Food Agency) -- Food safety regulations, hygiene rules, labelling requirements, LIVSFS
- **Jordbruksverket** (Swedish Board of Agriculture) -- Animal health, raw milk regulations, farm-level food production rules
- **EU Regulation 852/2004** -- General hygiene requirements for food business operators
- **EU Regulation 853/2004** -- Specific hygiene rules for food of animal origin
- **LIVSFS** (Livsmedelsverkets forfattningssamling) -- Swedish food regulation series

Data is derived from Swedish public records and EU official publications.

## Currency

Food safety regulations change. New product requirements, labelling rules, and hygiene standards are introduced. This tool shows the data available at the time of its last ingestion run. Run `check_data_freshness` to see the age of the data.

## Jurisdiction

This server covers Sweden (SE) only. EU food safety regulations are reflected through their Swedish implementation (Livsmedelsverket/LIVSFS), not directly.
